# Nuts and Bolts of Computational Methods

Welcome to the foundational lesson of Computational Methods in Electrical and Electronic Engineering. This lesson establishes the core concepts you'll need to effectively use numerical computing in your engineering practice.

## Why Computational Methods?

Modern electrical engineering problems are often too complex for analytical solutions:
- **Circuit simulation** with thousands of components
- **Electromagnetic field analysis** in 3D structures
- **Signal processing** with massive datasets
- **Control system design** with nonlinear dynamics

Computational methods bridge the gap between mathematical theory and practical engineering solutions.

## What You'll Learn

1. **Computational Thinking** — How to frame engineering problems for numerical solution
2. **Number Representation** — How computers store and manipulate numbers (and their limits)
3. **Error Analysis** — Understanding and controlling numerical errors
4. **Algorithm Basics** — Evaluating efficiency and choosing the right approach

## Prerequisites

- Basic programming experience (Python or MATLAB)
- Familiarity with linear algebra fundamentals
- Understanding of calculus concepts

## Tools We'll Use

Throughout this module, we'll primarily use:
- **Python** with NumPy, SciPy, and Matplotlib
- **MATLAB** as an alternative for certain demonstrations

> **Tip:** Install Anaconda or use Google Colab to get started quickly with Python.

## The Computational Engineering Mindset

As a computational engineer, you'll learn to:
1. **Model** physical systems mathematically
2. **Discretize** continuous problems into discrete representations
3. **Solve** using appropriate numerical algorithms
4. **Validate** results against known solutions or experiments
5. **Optimize** for accuracy, speed, and memory usage
