# Exam Focus — MOSFET Physical Structure

## What Examiners Test

MOSFET structure questions typically appear as **short-answer or diagram-labelling** questions worth 3–6 marks. They're considered "easy marks" but students often lose points on the details.

## Common Question Types

### Type 1: Label the Structure (3–4 marks)
You'll be given a cross-sectional diagram of a MOSFET and asked to label the terminals, regions, and layers. Key labels examiners expect:
- Four terminals: Gate (G), Drain (D), Source (S), Body (B)
- Oxide layer (SiO₂) with thickness $t_{ox}$
- n⁺ source and drain regions
- p-type substrate
- Channel region (length $L$, width $W$)

**Time: 2–3 minutes.** Don't overthink — label clearly and move on.

### Type 2: Compare BJT and MOSFET (4–6 marks)
A table comparison is the fastest way to score full marks. Always include:
- Control mechanism (current vs voltage)
- Input impedance
- Gate/base current
- Key advantage of each

### Type 3: Explain the MOS Capacitor (3–4 marks)
"Explain how the MOS structure forms a channel." Answer must include:
1. Gate voltage creates electric field through oxide
2. Field repels majority carriers (holes) from surface
3. Minority carriers (electrons) accumulate → inversion layer
4. Inversion layer = conductive channel between S and D

## Common Mistakes
- Confusing the body terminal with ground (body is the substrate, not necessarily ground)
- Forgetting that the gate draws **zero DC current**
- Mixing up channel length $L$ (S-to-D distance) with channel width $W$ (lateral extent)
- Not mentioning the oxide layer when explaining why $i_G = 0$

## Mark Allocation Tip
If the question says "Draw **and** label", you must do both. A correct diagram without labels, or correct labels on a sloppy diagram, will cost you half the marks.
