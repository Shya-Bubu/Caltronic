# Exam Focus — Depletion-Mode MOSFETs

## What Examiners Test

Depletion-mode questions appear as comparison/short-answer (3–5 marks) or as part of MOS inverter circuit analysis.

## Common Question Types

### Type 1: Enhancement vs Depletion Comparison (4–6 marks)
Table comparing: default state, VT sign, turn-ON/OFF conditions, IDSS concept.

### Type 2: Calculate IDSS (2–3 marks)
Given K and VT: $I_{DSS} = (K/2)V_T^2$

### Type 3: Depletion Load in MOS Inverter (4–6 marks)
Explain why a depletion MOSFET with gate tied to source acts as a constant-current load. Key: VGS = 0 → always ON at IDSS.

## Common Mistakes
- Saying depletion-mode devices can't operate with positive VGS (they can — it enhances the channel further)
- Confusing the sign of VT (negative for n-channel depletion, positive for p-channel depletion)
- Forgetting IDSS as a datasheet parameter
