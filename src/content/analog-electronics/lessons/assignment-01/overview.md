# BJT Tutorial — Complete Guide & Solutions

> This is your **complete preparation guide** for EE2040 Tutorial 1. By the time you finish this lesson, you'll be able to solve every single question on the assignment — not by memorising answers, but by genuinely understanding how BJT circuits work.

## What You'll Learn

This lesson takes you from **zero BJT knowledge** to confidently analysing multi-stage amplifiers with feedback:

1. **BJT fundamentals** — how the transistor works, what the three terminals do, and the three regions of operation
2. **DC biasing** — how to set the operating point and why bias stability matters
3. **DC analysis and design** — calculating Q-points, designing resistor values, using 5% standard resistors
4. **h-parameter model** — the two-port network representation of a transistor for AC analysis
5. **Small-signal equivalent circuits** — how to replace a BJT circuit with its AC model
6. **Single-stage amplifier analysis** — deriving voltage gain, current gain, input/output impedance
7. **Load lines and graphical analysis** — DC and AC load lines, waveform construction
8. **Multi-stage amplifiers and feedback** — cascading stages, loading effects, feedback topologies

Then, **six fully worked tutorial solutions** that apply everything you've learned — with every step explained, every assumption stated, and every calculation shown.

## How to Use This Lesson

**Read concepts 1-8 first.** They build on each other — don't skip ahead. Then read the solutions (concepts 9-11) and try to solve each question yourself before looking at the worked solution.

## Prerequisites

- Basic circuit theory (KVL, KCL, Ohm's law)
- Complex numbers and phasors (for AC analysis)
- Understanding of semiconductor junctions (you learned this last semester — we'll refresh everything you need)
