## Exam Focus

For this part of the lesson, the examiner is usually checking whether you understand the language of frequency response before moving to specific systems.

You should be able to define

$$
H(\omega)=H(s)\big|_{s=j\omega}
$$

and explain in words what $|H(\omega)|$ and $\angle H(\omega)$ mean. A good answer does not stop at the formulas. It states that the magnitude tells you the amplitude scaling of a sinusoidal steady-state output, while the phase tells you the phase shift produced by the system at that frequency.

You should also be ready to write the dB form

$$
20\log_{10}|H(\omega)|
$$

and explain why positive dB corresponds to amplification and negative dB corresponds to attenuation.

Common mistakes in this part include:

- writing $H(\omega)$ without connecting it to $H(s)$ evaluated at $s=j\omega$
- treating magnitude response and phase response as the same information
- forgetting that Bode plots are drawn against frequency
- stating the dB formula but not interpreting it

If a short descriptive question appears, the safest structure is:

1. define the frequency response
2. define magnitude and phase response
3. explain what a Bode plot contains
4. mention that the curves may be derived analytically or measured experimentally

That sequence matches the order of the note and usually gives a complete answer with no wasted writing.
