# Exam Focus - Why Routh-Hurwitz Is Needed

## What examiners ask here

This concept is often tested through short theory questions:

- state why equal-sign coefficients are necessary for stability
- explain why that condition is not sufficient
- describe what Routh-Hurwitz is designed to determine

## Good answer pattern

Start with the correct logic:

1. stable denominator -> same-sign coefficients is necessary
2. same-sign coefficients alone do not guarantee stability
3. therefore we need a stronger necessary-and-sufficient test

Then state that Routh-Hurwitz provides that test directly from the denominator coefficients.

## Common mistakes

- saying equal-sign coefficients are sufficient
- discussing numerator roots instead of denominator roots
- describing Routh-Hurwitz as a root-finding method rather than a stability test

## One sentence worth memorizing

> The Routh-Hurwitz criterion is a necessary-and-sufficient coefficient-based test for stability, used when direct factorization of the denominator polynomial is inconvenient.
