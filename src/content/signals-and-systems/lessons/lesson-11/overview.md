# Routh-Hurwitz Stability Criterion - Overview

This lesson stays on stability, but now the note moves from explicit pole calculation to coefficient-based testing.

The lecture has two parts:

- an active RLC example where the parameter $A$ changes the pole locations
- the Routh-Hurwitz test for deciding stability from the denominator coefficients

The main flow is

$$
\text{parameter change} \rightarrow \text{pole movement} \rightarrow \text{need for Routh test}
$$

and then

$$
\text{denominator coefficients} \rightarrow \text{Routh array} \rightarrow \text{stability result}
$$
