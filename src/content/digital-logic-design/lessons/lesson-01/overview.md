# Processing of Logical Information

> Welcome to digital logic design — the foundation of every computer, phone, and embedded system you'll ever build or use.

## What You'll Learn

This lesson takes you from the simplest building blocks of digital circuits — individual logic gates — all the way to designing, expressing, and minimising combinational circuits. By the end, you'll be able to:

1. **Identify and use the seven fundamental gates** (NOT, OR, AND, XOR, NAND, NOR, XNOR) and understand their transistor/diode-level implementations.
2. **Apply derived operations** like XOR to build adders, comparators, and parity checkers.
3. **Design combinational circuits** from a word-level specification through truth tables to gate-level netlists.
4. **Express circuits in canonical forms** — Sum of Products (SOP) using minterms and Products of Sum (POS) using maxterms.
5. **Simplify Boolean expressions** using algebra rules, De Morgan's theorems, and introduce the concept of Hamming distance for systematic minimisation.

## Why This Matters

Every digital system — from a simple LED controller to a billion-transistor processor — is built from combinations of these gates. Understanding how real voltages map to logical 0s and 1s, how diodes form OR gates, and how transistors form inverters connects the abstract world of Boolean algebra to the physical circuits you'll design in lab.

## Prerequisites

No formal prerequisites — this is the starting point.
